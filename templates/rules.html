League Rules and Scoring

Scoring Overview

  • +4/3/2/1 points for 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th place respectively
  • +1 to the player who draws first blood (combat damage only)
  • +1 for casting your commander four or more times
  • +1 saving another player from elimination (max 1/opponent)
  • +1 killing anyone with commander damage (max 1 point)
  • -1 killing everyone in the same turn or going infinite
  • -1 attacking the player in sole possession of last place (in standings)
    • Only applies if 4+ players at table
    • Does not apply if they attack you first
    • If the player in last does more than 10 damage to an opponent, limitations are removed for that opponent.
  • -1 failing to cast/play your commander from the command zone
  • +1 to the player whose deck receives the most votes at the end of the game

Rules and Restrictions

  1. Points are accrued each match, with the sum of all matches being the season score
  2. At the end of each match, players will calculate points together and will agree upon totals.
  3. Any budget restrictions exclude commander cost and basic land cost.
  4. Flex Cash Cards: If a budget includes flex, you receive an additional 50$ in flex cash to "buy" 3 cards. Whatever flex cash is leftover does not feed back into your deck costs.
    1. These slots exist to give players access to more expensive cards and help alleviate budget restraints and allow for the inclusion of a few 10-15$ cards in decks.
    2. Further, these slots facilitate the inclusion of fetch lands and tutors which are prohibitively expensive. Fetch lands and tutors chosen to fill flex slots cost 15$ towards your flex card budget.
  5. Budget / Deck costs are determined by Deckstats.net.
  6. Themes must be agreed upon by ⅔ majority to be enforced.
  7. A player cannot repeat a color as defined by the below Color Repetition definition*
    1. Repetition only counts for consecutive games
    2. For the purposes of “color” definition, colorless IS a color (ex: eldrazi decks)
    3. Repetition is based on the number of colors played in the prior game:
      1. 1 color prior means you can’t play that color, except in 3/4/5 color decks
      2. 2 colors prior means you can’t play either of those colors in a mono or duo combo, except in 4/5 color decks. You also cannot play both colors in a 3 color deck.
      3. 3 colors prior means you can’t play any two combo of those colors, except in a 4/5 color deck
      4. 4/5 colors prior means you have to play a mono or 2 color deck.
